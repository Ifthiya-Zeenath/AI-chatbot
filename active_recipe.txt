How about we make a beautiful, creamy **Parippu Hodi (Sri Lankan Dhal Curry)**? It's simple, full of flavour, and good for the soul. Don't worry, Aththamma will guide you through every step, nice and slow.

Here is everything you need, my dear:

---

### 📋 Ingredients

*   **1 cup** red lentils (masoor dhal)
*   **1 large** onion, finely sliced
*   **2-3** green chillies, slit lengthwise (adjust to your spice preference, putha)
*   **3-4 cloves** garlic, crushed
*   **1 sprig** curry leaves
*   **1 small piece** pandan leaf (rampe), about 1 inch
*   **1/2 teaspoon** fenugreek seeds (uluhal)
*   **1/2 teaspoon** turmeric powder
*   **1/2 teaspoon** chilli powder (optional, for a little warmth)
*   **1 cup** thin coconut milk
*   **1/2 cup** thick coconut milk
*   **1/2 teaspoon** salt (or to taste)
*   **1 cup** water

---

### 🔥 Stove Setup & Prep

1.  **Wash the lentils:** Rinse the red lentils under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
2.  **Chop & Crush:** Slice the onion thinly. Slit the green chillies. Crush the garlic cloves.
3.  **Gather Spices:** Have your curry leaves, pandan leaf, fenugreek, turmeric, and chilli powder (if using) ready.
4.  **Coconut Milk:** Measure out your thin and thick coconut milk.

---

### 👩‍🍳 Cooking Steps

1.  **Combine Ingredients:** In a medium pot, add the washed lentils, sliced onion, green chillies, crushed garlic, curry leaves, pandan leaf, fenugreek seeds, turmeric powder, chilli powder (if using), and salt.
2.  **Add Liquids:** Pour in the **1 cup of water** and the **1 cup of thin coconut milk**.
3.  **Bring to Boil:** Place the pot on the stove over **medium-high heat**. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil.
4.  **Simmer & Soften:** Reduce heat to **medium-low**. Cover the pot and simmer for **15-20 minutes**, or until the lentils are soft and mushy. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
5.  **Add Thick Milk:** Once lentils are soft, pour in the **1/2 cup of thick coconut milk**.
6.  **Gentle Heat:** Stir gently. Let it heat through for another **2-3 minutes** without boiling, just until it's warm and creamy.
7.  **Taste & Adjust:** Taste the curry and adjust salt if needed.
8.  **Serve Warm:** Remove from heat and serve immediately with rice or roti.

---

**Aththamma's Tip:** Ah, putha, remember that cooking is an act of love. Don't rush it. Taste as you go, adjust the spices to what your heart desires. And always, always cook with a happy heart. That's the secret ingredient for the most delicious food!
