Ah, hello my dear child! It warms my heart to hear from you. Are you hungry? Aththamma has just the thing to make your tummy happy and your heart warm. Let's make a lovely, creamy Sri Lankan Parippu Hodi, a simple Red Lentil Curry. It's so comforting, just like a hug!

Here is how we make it, step by step:

---

### 📋 Ingredients

*   1 cup red lentils (masoor dhal), washed
*   3 cups water (for cooking lentils)
*   1 small onion, finely sliced
*   2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise (adjust to your spice preference)
*   1 sprig fresh curry leaves
*   1-inch piece pandan leaf (rampe), optional but lovely
*   ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
*   ½ teaspoon fenugreek seeds (ulu haal)
*   ½ teaspoon mustard seeds
*   1 tablespoon coconut oil (for tempering)
*   ½ cup thin coconut milk
*   ½ cup thick coconut milk
*   Salt to taste

### 🔥 Stove Setup & Prep

*   Wash the red lentils thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear.
*   Slice the onion thinly, and slit the green chilies.
*   Gather all your spices and leaves.
*   Measure out your coconut milk into separate bowls.

### 👩‍🍳 Cooking Steps

1.  Combine washed lentils, water, onion, green chilies, curry leaves, pandan leaf, turmeric, and fenugreek seeds in a medium pot.
2.  Bring the mixture to a boil over **medium-high heat**.
3.  Reduce heat to **medium-low** and simmer gently.
4.  Cook until lentils are soft, about 15-20 minutes. Stir occasionally.
5.  Add the thin coconut milk and salt to taste.
6.  Simmer for another 5 minutes, do not boil vigorously.
7.  In a separate small pan, heat coconut oil over **medium heat**.
8.  Add mustard seeds to the hot oil.
9.  Once they splutter, immediately pour this tempering into the dhal curry.
10. Stir the tempering well into the curry.
11. Add the thick coconut milk to the pot.
12. Gently heat through for 2-3 minutes. Do **not** let it boil.
13. Remove from heat immediately.

---

**Aththamma's Tip:**
Ah, my dear child! A good dhal curry is like a warm hug. Always remember, never boil the thick coconut milk too much at the end, or it might curdle. Just a gentle warmth is enough to bring out its creamy goodness. Serve it with rice, and your heart will be full!
