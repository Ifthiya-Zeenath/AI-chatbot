Ah, putha! Kiri Bath, the most special dish for any celebration! Aththamma will guide you, don't you worry. We will make it perfect, just like how your *amma* used to make it. Here is everything you need to know, from start to finish:

---

### 📋 Ingredients

*   **2 cups** raw white rice (preferably short-grain, like Samba or Keeri Samba)
*   **4 cups** water (for cooking the rice)
*   **1 can (400ml)** thick coconut milk (or 1.5 cups freshly squeezed thick coconut milk)
*   **1/2 teaspoon** salt (or to taste)
*   **A little extra water** (if needed)

---

### 🔥 Stove Setup & Prep

1.  **Wash the rice:** Rinse the raw rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
2.  **Prepare coconut milk:** If using fresh, squeeze the thick milk. If canned, shake well.
3.  **Clear your workspace:** Make sure you have a clean, flat surface ready for spreading the Kiri Bath later.

---

### 👩‍🍳 Cooking Steps

1.  **Combine rice and water:** In a heavy-bottomed pot, add the washed rice and **4 cups of water**.
2.  **Bring to a boil:** Place the pot on the stove over **medium-high heat**.
3.  **Cover and simmer:** Once boiling, reduce heat to **low**, cover the pot tightly.
4.  **Cook the rice:** Simmer for **15-20 minutes**, or until water is absorbed and rice is almost cooked.
5.  **Check for doneness:** Rice should be tender but still a little firm.
6.  **Add coconut milk and salt:** Pour in the **thick coconut milk** and **salt**.
7.  **Stir gently:** Mix well, ensuring the coconut milk coats all the rice.
8.  **Continue simmering:** Cover again and simmer on **very low heat** for another **5-7 minutes**.
9.  **Stir occasionally:** Stir gently to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.
10. **Check consistency:** The Kiri Bath should be thick, creamy, and the rice fully cooked.
11. **Remove from heat:** Take the pot off the stove.
12. **Spread the Kiri Bath:** Immediately transfer the hot Kiri Bath onto a flat, greased tray or plate.
13. **Press firmly:** Using a spatula or the back of a spoon, press down to form an even, flat layer.
14. **Cool slightly:** Let it cool for **10-15 minutes** until it sets a bit.
15. **Cut into diamonds:** With a sharp, wet knife, cut into traditional diamond or square shapes.
16. **Serve warm:** Enjoy your beautiful Kiri Bath!

---
**Aththamma's Tip:** Always use a heavy-bottomed pot, putha. It stops the rice from sticking and burning, ensuring your Kiri Bath is perfectly creamy and soft, just like a mother's hug. Don't rush the simmering!
